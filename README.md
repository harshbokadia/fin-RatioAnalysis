# Financial Report Analysis: Gland Pharma, Indbank, and PNB Housing

## Project Overview
This report provides a detailed financial analysis and comparative study of three prominent Indian companies: **Gland Pharma Limited**, **Indbank Merchant Banking Services Limited**, and **PNB Housing Finance Limited**. The project evaluates their financial health by examining financial statements, key performance indicators, and market trends to provide valuable insights for investors and analysts.

## Companies Analyzed

### PNB Housing Finance Limited
Established in 1988 as a Punjab National Bank subsidiary, it provides home loans and non-housing financial solutions across India.

### Indbank Merchant Banking Services Ltd
A subsidiary of Indian Bank founded in 1989, it offers specialized services including issue management, portfolio management, and corporate advisory.

### Gland Pharma Limited
Founded in 1978 and headquartered in Hyderabad, Gland Pharma is a leading pharmaceutical manufacturer specializing in oncology medications and sterile injectables.

## Analysis Methodology
The analysis uses a robust framework of financial ratios categorized into four key areas:

* **Liquidity Ratios:** Current Ratio, Acid-Test (Quick) Ratio, Cash Ratio, and Operating Cash Flow Ratio.
* **Leverage Ratios:** Debt Ratio, Debt-to-Equity (D/E), Interest Coverage Ratio, and Debt Service Coverage Ratio.
* **Efficiency Ratios:** Asset Turnover, Inventory Turnover, and Receivables Turnover.
* **Profitability Ratios:** Gross Margin, Operating Margin, Return on Assets (ROA), and Return on Equity (ROE).
* **Market Value Ratios:** Book Value per Share, Earnings per Share (EPS), and P/E Ratio.

## Key Findings by Company

### Gland Pharma
* **Strongest Financial Position:** Identified as the strongest among the three analyzed firms.
* **Growth:** Showed consistent improvement in liquidity and a definite increase in Earnings Per Share.
* **Efficiency:** Features the fastest inventory turnover and cash conversion cycles.

### Indbank
* **Conservative Structure:** Maintains a **Debt-to-Equity ratio of 0**, relying entirely on its own capital and assets rather than borrowings.
* **Liquidity:** While showing good liquidity, it experienced a decrease in current ratio over the study period.
* **Asset Efficiency:** Demonstrated an increase in asset turnover over time.

### PNB Housing
* **Resource Management:** Demonstrated wise usage of resources over the years.
* **Liquidity Challenges:** Historically faced lower liquidity levels with an average current ratio below 1.0.
* **Deleveraging:** Successfully decreased its reliance on borrowings over time.

---

## Comparative Analysis
* **Liquidity Trends:** Gland Pharma and PNB have shown continuous gains in liquidity, while Indbank has trended downward.
* **Interest Coverage:** Indbank and PNB maintain constant and lower coverage ratios compared to Gland Pharma's fluctuating but higher levels.
* **Inventory:** As financial service providers, PNB and Indbank maintain zero inventory turnover, whereas Gland Pharma manages a complex physical goods cycle.
